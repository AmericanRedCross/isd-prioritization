ISD Prioritization

The GIS team investigated a number of factors that affect how suitable different countries are for ISD involvement and assistance. Our analysis focused on each country’s level of need, the availability of external country-specific funding, and the ease of ISD entry and operations.

Need includes natural disaster risk, vulnerability, lack of coping capacity, and population.

Funding includes ODA and country-specific humanitarian funding.

Ease includes the presence of an IFRC country office, number of ISD staff in-country, number of previous ARC disaster deployments, and the lack of human conflict.

High-conflict countries and OFAC-restricted countries have been removed from analysis. Data were turned into a weighted index. In each map, darker color shading indicates higher values and greater suitability for ISD program activities.

Natural disasters - 2016 projected disaster risk
Data source: 2016 INFORM database v0.2.25.

Vulnerability - Includes socioeconomics and vulnerable groups.
Data source: 2016 INFORM database v0.2.25.

Lack of Coping Capacity - Includes institutional support and existing infrastructure
Data source: 2016 INFORM database v0.2.25.

Population - 2013, in millions
Data source: OECD and World Bank. 2013 was the most recent year for which data were available at the time of map production. DAC countries have been excluded from this map.

Official Development Assistance - 2012-2013 average, in billions USD
Data source: OECD and World Bank. 2013 was the most recent year for which data were available at the time of map production. DAC countries have been excluded from this map.

Presence of IFRC Office - Indicates ease of entry for ARC operations
Data source: ARC, IFRC. Data are accurate as of December 2015.

Previous Emergency Deployments - ARC staff deployed, 2009-2015
Data source: ARC. Data includes emergency deployments from 2009-2015, inclusive.

Existing ISD Staff - Number of current staff
Data source: ARC. Data are accurate as of Dec 2015.

Lack of Human Conflict - Includes current and projected levels
Data source: 2016 INFORM database v0.2.25.

ISD FY16 Programs - Programs being implemented in fiscal year 2016
Data source: ARC. Data are accurate as of 18 Dec 2015. Note that several countries have FY16 programs but were excluded from the analysis for other reasons (such as DAC countries or high conflict). These include: Bahamas, Barbados, Central African Republic, Mexico, Russia, Somalia, Trinidad and Tobago, Uganda, and Ukraine.
